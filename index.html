Alexander Ku

I'm a PhD student in the Computational Cognitive Science Lab at Princeton University and a research scientist at Google DeepMind. I received my bachelors and masters in computer science from UC Berkeley.

My research is in computational cognitive science, computational linguistics, and machine learning. I'm interested in quantifying the degree to which natural language contributes to human and artificial intelligence. In particular, the role of language in higher-order cognitive functions that aren't primarily linguistic in nature (e.g., abstract reasoning, planning); with the goal being both a computational (i.e., Bayesian) and mechanistic level understanding.

Previously, I've worked on grounded language learning, (text-to-)image generation, and computational genomics.
