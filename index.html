Alexander Ku

I am a PhD student at Princeton, working with Tom Griffiths and Jon Cohen, and a research scientist at Google DeepMind.

I study the computational principles that make human cognition flexible and efficient. Specifically, I'm interested in how the mind combines familiar concepts to solve unfamiliar problems (flexibility), how those concepts are represented and processed, and how these representations evolve to make reasoning about familiar problems more efficient (efficiency). My work focuses on resource-rationality -- how people allocate limited cognitive resources to balance flexibility and efficiency -- and how similar principles might apply to large language models.

Research interests: resource-rationality, reasoning, metacognition, continual learning, cognitive control, automaticity
