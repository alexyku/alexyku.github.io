Alex's suggested reading

Note these are totally optional and focussed on cognitive science more broadly than development in particular. They also tend to emphasize computation and theory, as those are my interests.
  • First chapter of David Marr's vision which lays out the "levels of analysis" for studying the mind as an information processing system (whether that be a human or a computer).
  • Bayes in the age of intelligent machines is a contemporary take on Marr's levels and demonstrates how we can use the tools of cognitive science understand modern AI systems.
  • Here is a paper I worked on using a developmental psychology paradigm to probe for numerosity in AI systems.
  • This is an excellent essay written by ex-PNI professor Matt Botvnick on mind and consciousnous, and what that means in the modern age.
  • Still looking for more? Check out Sam Gershman's reading list.
